Framed for this terrible crime, Mario must\nclean the island, find the true culprit, and reclaim the\nShine Sprites—Delfino's sunny energy source—that\nhave hidden themselves to escape the mess.\nUsing the power of the Nintendo GameCube system,\nthis game offered dazzling water effects and a slick\nset of moves for Mario through his new tool, FLUDD.
T_Overview_02	It's the night of the Star Festival, and Star Bits are\nfalling from the sky! As everyone celebrates, Bowser\nsuddenly appears and lifts the castle—and Princess\nPeach—into space, leaving Mario stranded in orbit!\nIt's here that Mario meets a curious star child, Luma,\nand a mysterious woman in blue...\nThis game introduced new ways to control Mario,\nlike pointing and shaking the Wii Remote, as he\nexplores miniature planets with fun forms of gravity.
T_Overview_03	The Nintendo 64 system gave this Mario adventure\na more realistic instrumental sound than any prior\ngame in the Super Mario series. This soundtrack\nenhanced player experiences with arrangements\nthat smoothly transitioned between each 3D space.
T_Overview_04	Enjoy the bouncy, tropical sounds of Isle Delfino,\nreleased here for the first time! The ever-catchy\nDelfino Plaza theme boasts a bounty of variations.\nSome classic Mario melodies also turn up—like the\nunderground music from Super Mario Bros.,\nnewly arranged here as Shadow Mario's theme!
T_Overview_05	This soundtrack marks the first use of a full orchestra\nin the Super Mario series. The bright, bittersweet,\nand inspiring sounds of this score work to capture\nthe majesty of space exploration. These tracks were\noriginally released in Japan as a two-disc Club\nNintendo exclusive.
